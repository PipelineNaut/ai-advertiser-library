📂 Step 2: Install It

  1. Unzip the downloaded file — you'll see a folder called traffic-command-student-export
  2. Open VS Code with your my-ad-agency folder (from Lesson 5.2)
  3. Drag the entire contents of the unzipped folder into your workspace
  4. You should see folders: agents/, knowledge/, skills/, commands/ plus a README.md
  5. That's it. Your traffic team is loaded.

⚠️ If you customized your old CLAUDE.md (added client names, your voice preferences, etc.), don't delete it. The agents folder works alongside your existing CLAUDE.md — they add to it, they don't replace it.

🧠 What's Inside the Agent

Here's what your agent knows. You don't need to memorize any of this — the AI has it built in. But it helps to know what's under the hood.

📂 500+ Knowledge Files
Real campaign data, frameworks, and strategies from actual ad accounts. Andromeda paradigm, creative diversification, hook frameworks, niche benchmarks, scaling protocols. The agents pull from this knowledge base when answering your questions — so you're getting answers based on real results, not generic AI.
🛠️ 18 Skills
Plug-and-play capabilities: hook generation, copy generation, creative briefs, metric diagnosis, creative fatigue detection, funnel analysis, tracking audits, campaign structure, budget allocation, kill/scale rules, unit economics, and more. Each skill is a focused workflow the agents use automatically.
⚡ 5 Built-in Commands
Type these in Claude Code for instant workflows: /traffic (general traffic help), /traffic-audit (full campaign audit), /traffic-diagnose (diagnose a problem), /traffic-hooks (generate hooks), /traffic-scale (scaling plan).
📋 Templates
Ready-to-use templates for performance reports, creative briefs, campaign audits, scaling readiness checklists, and campaign launch checklists. Just ask and the agents fill them in with your actual data.
🔍 Diagnostic Framework
Zenith diagnoses problems automatically: High CPM + low CTR = creative problem (routes to Vortex). Good CTR + low conversions = landing page problem. Good apps + low shows = follow-up problem. It tells you what's broken, whose job it is to fix it, and what to do next.
