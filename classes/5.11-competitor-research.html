🔍 Step 1: Find Competitors in the Ad Library

📚 Meta Ad Library
facebook.com/ads/library — See every active ad any business is running. Free. No login needed.

Open the Ad Library

Go to facebook.com/ads/library. Set the country (usually United States) and category to "All ads."

Search for Competitors

Search by competitor name. If you don't know who they are, ask your client for their top 3-5. Or search the niche + city — "Dentist Tampa" shows every dental practice running ads in Tampa.

Grab 5-10 Ads

Mix of different competitors. Screenshot them, copy the text, or save the shareable link from the library. Get a variety — images, videos, carousels.

👀 Step 2: What to Look For

When reviewing competitor ads, pay attention to these six things:

⏱️ How Long Has It Been Running?

60+ days = probably working. Just started = they're testing. Long-running ads are the ones to study.

🪝 What's the Hook?

The first line of text or first second of video. What are they leading with?

🎁 What's the Offer?

Free consultation? Discount? Free trial? Limited time? What's the carrot?

🖼️ What Format?

Image, video, carousel? What's the visual showing? Professional photos or UGC?

📍 What's the CTA?

Book now, learn more, call now, shop now? Where are they sending people?

🔗 What's the Landing Page?

Click through if you can. See where they're sending traffic. Form? Calendar? Phone number?

⚡ Step 3: Feed Them to Claude

Drop your screenshots or paste the ad copy into Claude Code and use this prompt:

i'm analyzing competitor ads for a [NICHE] client in [CITY]. here are 
[X] ads from their competitors. analyze each one and give me:

1. what hook they're using and why it might work
2. what offer they're making
3. who they're probably targeting
4. what emotional trigger they're using (fear, desire, social proof, urgency, curiosity)
5. what's weak about this ad
6. what angle they're NOT covering that we could use

then at the end, give me:
- the top 3 patterns you see across all these ads
- 3 gaps that none of them are covering
- 5 ad angles we should test that would stand out from all of these

Paste the ad copy below the prompt. Or reference the screenshots if you dropped them in your project folder.

📊 What Claude Gives You Back

Here's the kind of output you'll get:

Competitor A — SmileBright Dental

Leading with a fear hook — "Don't let dental problems get worse." Offering free exam + X-ray for new patients. Targeting adults 25-55 who've been avoiding the dentist. Using a stock photo of a smiling woman. Weak point: the hook is generic, the image looks like every other dental ad, and they bury the offer in paragraph 3.

Gap Found:

None of these competitors are showing the actual experience of visiting the practice. No behind-the-scenes, no doctor introductions, no "here's what your first visit actually looks like" content.

Recommended Angles:

1. "First visit" walkthrough that removes fear
2. Staff intro / personality-driven content
3. Price transparency ("here's what a cleaning actually costs")

You now have a competitive strategy in 5 minutes. Use the gaps and angles to write your client's ads.

💡 Save it. Store the analysis in clients/[client]/competitor-analysis.md so you can reference it when building campaigns and track changes over time.

📅 Monthly Competitor Check

Don't do this once and forget about it. Competitors change their ads, new ones show up, old ones disappear. Run this prompt once a month:

i'm doing a monthly competitor check for [CLIENT NAME]. 

check the meta ad library for these competitors: [LIST NAMES]

compare what they're running now vs what we saw last month 
(see clients/[client]/competitor-analysis.md for the previous analysis).

tell me:
- what's new
- what they stopped running (probably wasn't working)
- any new angles or offers we should be aware of
- anything we should adjust in our own strategy based on this

⚠️ Set a calendar reminder. First Monday of every month. "Competitor check for [client]." If you're not monitoring, you're flying blind while competitors evolve around you.

🚀 Pro Moves

Once you've got the basics down, level up with these:

🌎 Check Competitors in Other Cities

Your client's local competitors might all run the same boring ads. But a dentist in Miami might have a killer angle nobody in Tampa has seen. Steal ideas from other markets before they spread.

🏢 Look at the Big Players

National brands spend millions on testing. Search the big names in your client's niche. If a national chain has been running the same ad for 6 months, that angle works. Adapt it locally.

📈 Track Ad Longevity

Start a simple spreadsheet: competitor name, ad start date, still running yes/no. After a few months you'll see exactly which ad styles have staying power in your niche. That data is gold.

💡 Combine with Lead Scraping. Use the prospecting data from Lesson 5.9 to identify which competitors are running ads, then analyze those ads here. The two workflows feed each other.

🚫 What NOT to Do

🛑 Don't copy competitor ads. You're looking for patterns and gaps, not copy-paste material. If you run the same ad as a competitor, you're just adding noise. The whole point is to find what they're NOT doing.

🛑 Don't skip the analysis. Screenshotting 10 ads and never looking at them again is a waste of time. The value is in the Claude analysis — patterns, gaps, angles.

⚠️ Don't only look at your city. Local competitors often copy each other. Look at 2-3 other cities in the same niche for fresh angles that haven't saturated your market.
