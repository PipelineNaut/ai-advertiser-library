Lesson 3.2 — Module 3: Fulfillment

How Online Advertising Actually Works

Fulfillment Beginner ~10 min
Forget everything you think you know about ads. This lesson explains the entire thing using one example: a pizza shop. By the end, you'll be able to explain online advertising to any business owner in 60 seconds — and do the napkin math to show them why it's a no-brainer.

🍕 1. The Pizza Shop — One Analogy to Rule Them All

Imagine you're running ads for Tony's Pizza in Tampa. Tony's been around 15 years, makes a great pie, but his only marketing is a faded sign out front and some Yelp reviews from 2019. Here's how online ads work for Tony:

💰
Ad Spend / Budget

Paying for flyers on doors

Tony gives you $10/day. That's like paying someone to put flyers on doors. More money = more doors. But instead of random doors, Meta puts your flyer on the phones of people who actually order pizza.

🎯
Targeting / Audience

Choosing WHICH doors

You don't put flyers on every door in Tampa. You pick: within 5 miles of Tony's, people aged 25-55, who've ordered food delivery before, who like Italian food. That's targeting. You're choosing who sees the ad.

🎨
Creative / Ad

The flyer itself

The photo of the pizza. The headline ("Family Deal: 2 Large Pizzas, $24.99"). The coupon. That's your creative. A boring flyer gets thrown away. A good one makes them hungry and call.

👆
Click / Engagement

Someone picks up the flyer

Out of 500 people who see your ad, maybe 15-25 actually tap on it. That's a click. They're interested enough to look closer. Now they're on Tony's page or website.

📞
Conversion / Lead

Someone calls and orders

Out of those 15-25 clicks, maybe 3-5 people actually call, order online, or fill out a form. That's a conversion. That's money in Tony's register. That's what you're paid to deliver.

📊
ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)

Did Tony make more than he spent?

Tony spent $10 on flyers. He got 3 orders worth $80 total. That's 8x ROAS. For every $1 Tony spent, he got $8 back. That's the number that keeps Tony paying you every month.

🔍 2. Where Tony's $10 Actually Goes

When Tony gives you $10/day to spend on ads, here's exactly what happens to that money:

$10
Tony's daily ad budget
This goes straight to Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
↓
~$3
Meta's cut (platform profit)
Meta keeps ~30% as profit. That's their business model. The rest goes to showing your ad.
↓
500–1,000
People see Tony's ad (impressions)
Your ad shows up in their Instagram feed or Facebook. Most scroll past. That's normal.
↓
15–25
People tap on the ad (clicks)
2-5% click rate is normal. These people are interested. They land on Tony's page or website.
↓
3–5
People take action (conversions)
They call, order online, book an appointment, or fill out a form. This is what you're measured on.
↓
$80+
Revenue generated for Tony
3 pizza orders × ~$27 average = $80. Tony spent $10, made $80. That's 8x return. Tony's happy. You get paid.

Key insight: Most of the people who see the ad won't click. Most people who click won't convert. That's normal. Your job is to make the numbers work so Tony makes more than he spends. If he does, he pays you forever.

🗣️ 5. The 60-Second Explanation (Use This on Calls)

"Here's how it works in simple terms. I put an ad for your business on Facebook and Instagram. That ad shows up on the phone of people in your area who are already looking for what you sell. When they see it, they tap on it and either call you, book online, or fill out a form. You get the customer, you make money. My job is to make sure you're getting way more back than you're spending on the ads. Most of our clients see a 5 to 10x return — meaning for every dollar they put in, they get five to ten dollars back. And I handle everything. You just answer the phone."

Memorize this. Practice it until it sounds natural. This is how you explain ads to a business owner who's never run one. No jargon. No confusion. Just "I put your ad in front of the right people and you get customers."

📖 6. Jargon → English Cheat Sheet

You'll hear these terms in Ads Manager. Here's what they mean in plain English:

JARGON
WHAT IT ACTUALLY MEANS
Impression
Someone saw your ad (scrolled past it)
CTR (Click-Through Rate)
% of people who tapped the ad. 2-5% is normal.
CPM
Cost per 1,000 views. How expensive it is to show your ad. $5-15 is normal for local.
CPC (Cost Per Click)
How much each tap costs you. $0.50-$3 is normal for local.
CPL (Cost Per Lead)
How much each phone call / form fill costs. $5-25 for local businesses.
CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)
How much a paying customer costs you. This is the number that matters most.
ROAS
Return on ad spend. Spent $100, made $500 = 5x ROAS. Above 3x is good for most local businesses.
Pixel
A tracking code on the website that tells Meta "this person bought / called / booked."
Learning Phase
First 3-7 days where Meta figures out who responds to your ad. Results are messy. Don't panic.
