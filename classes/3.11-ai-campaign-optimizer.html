Campaign Optimization — Your Daily 15 Minutes

Optimization Daily Routine ~15 min
Your campaigns are live. Now your job changes: you're not building anymore, you're maintaining. Every day, for every client, you spend 15 minutes reading the data and making decisions. No guessing. No feelings. Just numbers → rules → action. This is where you earn your retainer.

🖥️ Screens to Open

1. Ads Manager — Campaign View — Where you'll spend most of your time 2. Ads Manager — Ad Level — Where you see individual ad performance

⏱️ 1. The 15-Minute Daily Routine

This is the same routine every day. No thinking about what to do — just follow the steps. Set a timer if you have to. 15 minutes, no more.

🖥️ SHOW ON SCREEN: Open Ads Manager. Walk through each step of the routine in real time with Tony's Pizza as the example. Students need to see this takes 15 minutes, not 2 hours.
0:00 – 3:00
👀 SCAN
Open Ads Manager. Look at yesterday's numbers. What changed? Any big spikes or drops? Just observe — don't touch anything yet.
3:00 – 7:00
🎯 DECIDE
Go ad by ad. Apply the decision rules (Section 3). Label each one: KILL, WATCH, SCALE, or REFRESH. Write it down.
7:00 – 12:00
⚡ EXECUTE
Now make the changes. Kill losers. Bump winner budgets 20%. Add new creatives if frequency is high. Only do what you decided — nothing extra.
12:00 – 15:00
📝 LOG
Write down what you changed and why. Keep a simple note — date, what you did, reason. This becomes your weekly client report.

The #1 beginner mistake: spending 2 hours "optimizing" and making things worse. The learning phase needs ~50 conversions per week to stabilize. Every time you change something, it resets. Do LESS. Only change what the rules tell you to change.

🎯 3. The 4 Decision Rules

Every ad gets ONE of four labels. No "maybe." No "let me think about it." These rules remove emotion from your decisions:

KILL
CPA is 3x+ your target
This ad is bleeding money. Turn it off immediately. Don't hope it gets better — it won't. You need at least 2-3x your target CPA in spend before judging (so the data is real).
Target $10 → Kill at $30+
WATCH
CPA is 1.5x-3x your target
Not great, not terrible. Give it 2-3 more days of data. If it doesn't improve → kill. If it drops below target → scale.
Target $10 → Watch at $15-30
SCALE
CPA is at or below target
Winner. Increase budget by 20-30%. Don't double it overnight — gradual scaling prevents the learning phase from resetting.
Target $10 → Scale at ≤$10
REFRESH
Frequency hits 3.0+
People are seeing the same ad too many times. Performance will drop soon. Add 3-5 new creatives to the ad set. Don't pause the old ones yet — let Meta redistribute.
Freq 3.0+ → Add creatives

Print these rules. Stick them next to your monitor. When you're tempted to make a "gut feeling" change — look at the rules. If the data doesn't say to change it, don't change it. The algorithm is smarter than your gut. Always.

🚫 5. The 5 Optimization Killers

These mistakes will cost your clients money and reset the learning phase. Every beginner makes at least one of these. Don't be that person:

1
Changing things during learning phase
Meta's algorithm needs ~50 conversions per week to figure out who to show your ads to. If you edit budget, targeting, or creative before that — it resets to zero and starts learning all over again. Wait.
2
Doubling budget overnight
$15/day → $30/day = learning phase resets. The algorithm panics when you change too much at once. Scale 20-30% at a time: $15 → $18 → $22 → $27. Be patient.
3
Panic-killing ads after 24 hours
Day 1 data means nothing. You need the ad to spend at least 2-3x your target CPA before judging. If target CPL is $10, let the ad spend at least $20-30 before you decide it's a loser.
4
Editing the winning ad
An ad is performing well → "let me tweak the copy to make it even better" → you just killed it. NEVER edit a winner. If you want to test a variation, create a NEW ad. Leave the winner completely alone.
5
Optimizing based on feelings
"I don't like this ad" doesn't matter. "This ad has 3x CPA after $30 spend" matters. You are not the target audience. Your client is not the target audience. Only the data decides what stays and what goes.

📋 7. The Weekly Client Report

Every week, send your client a simple update. Not a 10-page document — just the numbers that matter and what you did. Copy this template:

📋 Weekly Report Template
Hey [Client Name]! Here's your weekly ads update: 📊 THIS WEEK'S NUMBERS • Total Spend: $___ • Total Leads: ___ • Cost Per Lead: $___ • Best Performing Ad: [name] — $[CPL] 🟢 WHAT'S WORKING • [Top ad and WHY it's winning — angle, creative type] • [Any trends you noticed] 🔴 WHAT WE CHANGED • Paused [X] underperforming ads (too expensive) • Increased budget on [Y] — best performer • Added [Z] new creatives to keep things fresh 📅 NEXT WEEK'S PLAN • [What you'll do next — new creatives, scaling, etc.] Questions? Let me know!

Send this every Monday. Clients don't care about CPM or CTR — they care about "how many leads did I get and how much did it cost?" Keep it simple. This one message keeps clients happy and paying every month.
