🧠 2. Why Broad Targeting Wins

This is the hardest thing for new media buyers to accept. It feels wrong to NOT target. But here's the math:

10,000x
More ad candidates Andromeda evaluates per auction
3.1B
Daily active users Meta can search through for your buyer
+22%
ROAS improvement with Advantage+ broad targeting
The Logic

When you add interest targeting, you're telling Meta: "Only show my ads to people who like [pizza]." But Meta already knows who likes pizza — and it knows a LOT more than the interest categories you can select.

By going broad, you're saying: "Here are 20 different ads with 20 different angles. Show them to whoever responds." The algorithm learns from every impression which PEOPLE respond to which CREATIVES — and it optimizes in real time.

Your creatives ARE the targeting. A pizza ad showing a family dinner targets families. The same pizza ad about late-night munchies targets college students. The algorithm figures this out automatically — faster and better than you ever could manually.

📍 3. The Only Targeting That Matters: Location

For local businesses, there's ONE targeting setting you actually need: location radius. A pizza shop in Miami doesn't need to reach people in New York. Everything else? Leave it broad.

🔄 4. The One Exception: Retargeting

There's one place where custom audiences still matter: retargeting people who already know the business. These are warm audiences — they visited the website, watched a video, or are on the customer list. They're cheaper to convert because they already have context.

🟢 Worth Setting Up
Website visitors (last 30-90 days)
Video viewers (watched 50%+)
Customer email list (upload to Meta)
Instagram/FB engagers (last 30 days)
Lead form openers (didn't submit)
🔴 Don't Bother
Interest-based audiences
Lookalike audiences
Behavior stacking
Detailed targeting expansion
Saved audiences from 2023

For most local biz clients on Day 1: They won't have website traffic or an email list yet. That's fine — start with broad + location only. Build the retargeting audiences over time as traffic comes in. Week 1 = cold. Week 4+ = retargeting layer on top.

🔍 5. Research the Audience, Don't Target Them

Here's where Nexus still matters. You don't use AI to build targeting settings — you use it to understand the audience so your creatives speak to them. The research feeds your copy and creatives, not your Ads Manager settings.

🖥️ SHOW ON SCREEN: Paste this prompt into Claude with "Tony's Pizza" as the example. Show the output. Point out how this feeds your AD COPY (lesson 3.8), not your targeting settings.
Nexus Audience Research Prompt
I'm running Facebook/Instagram ads for a local business. I need to deeply understand their target audience — NOT for targeting settings (I'm using broad targeting), but to write better ad copy and create better visuals. BUSINESS: [Business name] WHAT THEY SELL: [Product/service] LOCATION: [City, state] PRICE RANGE: [Average ticket] Give me: 1. The top 10 REASONS someone would buy this (not features — emotional motivations) 2. The top 5 OBJECTIONS they'd have before buying 3. The top 5 MOMENTS when they'd think about this product (triggers) 4. What language/slang does this audience actually use? 5. Who are they comparing this business to? (competitors + alternatives) 6. What does their day look like? (daily routine relevant to the product) Be specific. Use real examples. No generic marketing talk.

This research → your ad angles. Each "reason to buy" = an ad angle for Aurelius. Each "objection" = a BAB or PAS ad. Each "trigger moment" = a hook. You're not targeting pizza lovers — you're writing an ad that speaks to someone staring at an empty fridge at 7pm.
