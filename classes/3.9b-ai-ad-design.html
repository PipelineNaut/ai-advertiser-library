AI Ad Creative Design

Creatives Hands-On ~15 min
Your client has zero professional photos. No designer. No budget for a photoshoot. This lesson shows you how to create professional ad images for any local business in under 10 minutes using two free tools.

🎯 Why This Matters

Creative is the #1 lever in Meta ads right now. Under Meta's Andromeda system, targeting matters less than it used to. Meta's AI finds the right people. YOUR job is to make something good enough that people stop scrolling.

For local businesses, the creative has to feel real. Not corporate stock photo real. Like someone in your neighborhood took that picture. The good news: AI tools can generate exactly that in about 60 seconds.

⚡ The Workflow: Create an Ad in 10 Minutes

Know What You're Making

Before you open anything, answer 3 questions:

  • What does the client do? (roofing, dental, HVAC, etc.)
  • What's the offer? (free inspection, 20% off, free consultation)
  • Who's seeing this? (homeowners 30-55 in Dallas)

Also grab the client's logo and brand color from their website. Takes 30 seconds.

Generate the Design in Google Stitch

Go to stitch.withgoogle.com and describe your ad in plain English:

Design a Facebook ad image for a roofing company in Dallas. Show a clean, modern house with a new roof. Bright lighting, professional look. Include text that says "Free Roof Inspection" in bold at the top. Use dark blue as the primary color. Add a "Call Now" button at the bottom. 1080x1080 format.

Stitch generates the layout. If you don't like something, just tell it:

"Make the text bigger"
"Add a before-and-after split"
"Use warmer colors"
"Make it more professional"

It updates in real time. When you're happy, export the image.

Polish in Canva (2 minutes)

Open canva.com. Upload the Stitch export. Then:

  1. Drop in the client's actual logo
  2. Adjust any text if needed
  3. Pinch to preview at phone size (this is how 95% of people will see it)
  4. Download as PNG

🎯 5 Ad Angles That Work for Every Local Business

You don't need to reinvent the wheel. These 5 cover 90% of local business ads. For each one, open Stitch, describe it, export, polish in Canva.

1. The Direct Offer

Clean image of the service result. Offer front and center. No tricks.

"Free Roof Inspection — Book Today"
Works for: every niche

2. The Before/After

Split screen. Left: the problem. Right: the fix. Most powerful format for service businesses.

Left: old damaged roof → Right: brand new clean roof
Works for: roofing, dental, landscaping, cleaning, renovation, salon

3. The Social Proof

Star rating + review quote + professional image. Builds trust instantly.

"4,200+ Dallas Homeowners Trust Us" ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Works for: any established business with reviews

4. The Problem Call-Out

Image shows the problem. Ad copy offers the solution. Gets attention through pain.

"Your AC Bill Doesn't Have to Be This High"
Works for: HVAC, plumbing, auto repair, pest control

5. The Urgency / Seasonal

Limited time + seasonal relevance. Creates FOMO without being cheesy.

"Spring Special — 20% Off Before April 15"
Works for: every niche (just match the season)

💡 Pro tip: When testing ads, create one image for each of these 5 angles and let Meta figure out which one wins. Don't guess — test.

🚫 What NOT to Do

🛑 Don't use stock photos that look fake. Nobody believes the smiling man in a suit is your client's plumber. Use Stitch to generate something that looks real.

🛑 Don't put too much text on the image. 5-8 words max on the image itself. The rest goes in the ad copy above the image.

⚠️ Don't skip the client's brand. Grab their logo and brand color from their website before you start. A great ad that doesn't match their brand feels off.

⚠️ Don't forget mobile preview. 95% of people see the ad on their phone. If your text is too small to read on a phone, redo it.

🛑 Don't use before/after health claims without a disclaimer. Dental, weight loss, skincare — Meta flags these. Add "Results may vary" if using before/after for health-related businesses.
