👁️ 1. The 2-Second Rule

IF THEY CAN'T GET IT IN 2 SECONDS, THEY SCROLL
Your ad is competing against their friends, memes, news, and reels. You get 2 seconds. Maybe less.
✅ SCROLL STOPPERS
🟢 Human faces (especially eyes looking at camera)
🟢 Bold contrast (dark bg + bright text)
🟢 One clear message (not 5 things at once)
🟢 Real photos > stock photos
🟢 Numbers in the image ("$12.99" / "20 min")
🟢 Before/after visuals
🟢 Movement (video > static always)
❌ SCROLL PAST
🔴 Generic stock photos (handshake guy, pointing lady)
🔴 Too much text crammed in
🔴 Low contrast (grey on grey)
🔴 No focal point (where do I look?)
🔴 Business logo taking up 30% of the ad
🔴 Fancy fonts nobody can read
🔴 Looking like an ad (people scroll past "ads")
🖥️ SHOW ON SCREEN: Open your Instagram feed. Scroll. Point out which ads stop you and which you scroll past. Ask "why?"

🎨 2. The 6 Ad Formats You Need

Vortex checks that your creative mix has variety. You need multiple formats, not just multiple images. Here are the 6 you'll use for every client:

ESSENTIAL
🖼️
Single Image
One strong image + text overlay. The workhorse of Facebook ads. Quick to make, easy to test.
Tony's: Photo of a steaming pizza just out of the oven. Bold text: "$12.99 — Delivered in 20 min." Simple. Done.
HIGH CTR
📸
Carousel
2-10 swipeable cards. Tells a story or shows multiple products. Gets more engagement because people swipe.
Tony's: Card 1: "🍕 Friday Night?" → Card 2: Menu options → Card 3: "2 large + 4 drinks = $19.99" → Card 4: "Order Now"
BEST PERFORMER
🎬
Video (15-30s)
Outperforms static 9 out of 10 times. Hook in first 2 seconds. Show the product. End with CTA.
Tony's: Close-up of dough being tossed → cheese stretching → delivery at door → family eating → "$12.99" text card
TRUST BUILDER
🤳
UGC-Style
Looks like a real person filmed it on their phone. Not polished. That's the point — it feels authentic in the feed.
Tony's: Someone filming themselves opening the pizza box: "Yo I just found this place and... are you serious right now? This is $12.99??"
CONVERSION
↔️
Before / After
Split-screen or swipe comparison. Extremely powerful for services (dental, fitness, cleaning, landscaping).
Tony's: Left side: sad microwave meal, grey lighting. Right side: Tony's pizza on a nice table, family laughing. Same person, different dinner.
ENGAGEMENT
😂
Meme / Relatable
Humor + relatability = shares + saves. Doesn't look like an ad. Gets organic reach on top of paid.
Tony's: Drake meme format — "Cooking after work" (nah) vs "Tony's at the door in 20 min" (yeah). Caption: "You know the vibe 🍕"

Vortex's rule: every client needs at least 4 of these 6 formats. Not 15 single images. Not 10 videos. A MIX. The algorithm needs variety to test what resonates with different audience segments.

Restaurant / Food — Tony's Pizza Example
📸 WHAT TO SHOOT
Close-up food shots (cheese pull, steam, toppings). Real kitchen footage. Delivery moment (door opens). Happy customers eating. The chef making food.
🎨 VISUAL STYLE
Warm colors (orange, red, yellow). Dark backgrounds make food pop. Big price/deal text. Show the food FIRST — not the logo. Natural lighting.
🏆 TOP PERFORMING FORMATS
1. Video of food being made (UGC style) → 2. Carousel of menu items → 3. Single image with deal overlay → 4. Before/after (empty table → full pizza spread)
Dental — Smile Transformation
📸 WHAT TO SHOOT
Before/after smile photos (with permission). Clean office shots. Friendly staff. Patient testimonial videos. The dentist explaining procedures in plain English.
🎨 VISUAL STYLE
Clean whites and blues. Trust-building visuals. Show real people, real smiles. Avoid sterile/clinical look — make it warm and welcoming. Big clear offer text.
🏆 TOP PERFORMING FORMATS
1. Before/after smile carousel (HUGE engagement) → 2. Patient video testimonial → 3. "New patient special $99" single image → 4. Doctor explainer video (builds trust)
Gym / Fitness — Get In Shape
📸 WHAT TO SHOOT
Real members working out (not models). Transformation photos. Community moments (group classes, high fives). Clean facility shots. Trainer explaining exercises.
🎨 VISUAL STYLE
Bold, high energy. Dark backgrounds with bright accent colors. Action shots with motion blur. Strong typography. Show effort AND results. Avoid intimidating vibes for general gyms.
🏆 TOP PERFORMING FORMATS
1. Transformation video (before/after with story) → 2. Workout clip UGC → 3. "First week free" offer image → 4. Member testimonial carousel
Real Estate — Dream Home
📸 WHAT TO SHOOT
Property walkthrough videos. Neighborhood highlights. "Just listed" / "Just sold" graphics. Agent on camera giving market tips. Drone shots of the area.
🎨 VISUAL STYLE
Aspirational but attainable. Bright, clean photos. Show the lifestyle, not just the house. Price and location front and center. Professional but not corporate.
🏆 TOP PERFORMING FORMATS
1. Property walkthrough video (15-30s) → 2. "Just listed" carousel with room photos → 3. Market update single image → 4. "Homes under $X in [area]" lead ad
Home Services — Before/After Gold
📸 WHAT TO SHOOT
Before/after transformations (the #1 format). Time-lapse of work being done. Satisfying cleaning/pressure washing videos. Happy homeowner reactions. The truck/team arriving.
🎨 VISUAL STYLE
Raw and real beats polished. Phone footage > professional shoots. Split-screen before/after is king. Price + service area in bold. "Before" side should look genuinely messy.
🏆 TOP PERFORMING FORMATS
1. Before/after side-by-side (INSANE engagement) → 2. Satisfying time-lapse video → 3. "$99 first clean" offer image → 4. Homeowner reaction UGC

⚡ 6. The 45-Minute Creative Build

This is the exact process to go from zero creatives to 15-25 ready-to-upload ads in under an hour:

🖥️ SHOW ON SCREEN: Walk through this process live. Build 2-3 creatives in Canva as you explain. Show the Claude prompt for video scripts.
1
Pull angles from 3.8 — 5 min
Open your Aurelius output from lesson 3.8. You already have 12 ad copy variations with different angles. Each angle becomes a creative.
2
Check your swipe file — 5 min
Open your swipe file. Find formats that match this client's niche. A restaurant? Look at food ads you saved. A gym? Pull those transformation formats. Don't reinvent — adapt.
3
Build 8-10 static creatives in Canva — 20 min
Use templates. Swap text to match your copy. Use client's photos or stock. Export both sizes (1080x1080 + 1080x1920). Mix: 4 single images, 2 carousels, 2 before/after.
4
Build 3-5 video creatives — 15 min
Ask Claude for 15-second video scripts (hook → body → CTA). Use client footage, stock footage, or screen recordings. Edit in CapCut. Add captions and music. Done.
5
Run through Vortex checklist — 5 min
Use the diversity checker below. Make sure you have enough variety in formats, angles, and visual styles. If Vortex says you're short, go back and add more.
Total: ~45 minutes → 15-25 ready-to-upload creatives
A design agency would charge $1,000-$2,000 for this. You did it for free with AI + Canva.
