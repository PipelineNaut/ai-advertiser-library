🌀 5. MEGA DIGESTIVE — Turn Courses Into Permanent Knowledge

this is the system that makes your brain compound. you bought a course. you watched a webinar. you read a book. most of that knowledge evaporates within a week. the digestive system turns it into permanent, searchable knowledge that your agents use forever.

INGEST

SYNTHESIZE

🧬 8. Memory System — Your AI Stops Forgetting

ok so by default, claude has goldfish memory. you open a new chat tomorrow, it forgets everything you said today. that's useless for an agency where you're juggling 5+ clients, dozens of decisions, and ongoing campaigns.

claude-mem fixes this. it's a memory system that uses a vector database (chroma) to save important things from your sessions. next time you open claude, it pulls the relevant memories automatically. you ask "what's the deal with the med spa client we onboarded in march" and it pulls the memory and answers like it remembers. because it does.
WHAT IT REMEMBERS

Four memory types, organized properly

User — who you are, your role, your preferences. Saved once, applies forever.

Feedback — corrections you gave claude. "Don't use em dashes." "Stop suggesting refunds." Saves you from re-correcting the same thing every session.

Project — current state of work. Which client launched when, which campaign is winning, what the next decision is.

Reference — pointers to where info lives in external systems. "Client billing is in stripe." "Performance reports are in google drive."

WHY AGENCIES NEED IT

Without memory vs. with memory

Without: you re-explain your business every session. Claude forgets what worked for client A. Every onboarding starts from zero. Knowledge stays in your head, not the system.

With: claude remembers every client. Decisions compound. New team members get up to speed faster because the memory teaches them. Your agency gets smarter every week, not just every quarter.

🔑
The unlock: Memory + Knowledge Base + Agents = your AI gets smarter every single day you use it. After 6 months it knows your business better than your last hire did. That's not exaggeration. That's compounding.

🪝 10. Hooks — Auto-Magic In The Background

hooks are scripts that fire automatically when claude does certain things. think of them as your AI's reflexes. they run without you asking. they keep your brain organized, validated, and synced.

without hooks, you have to remember to link new chunks, validate frontmatter, save important learnings to memory, commit changes to git so nothing gets lost. you'll forget. every time.

with hooks, all of that happens automatically. the brain stays organized. knowledge compounds without your discipline.
HOOK 1

Auto-Link Chunks

Triggers when: you create a new file in .brain/domains/

What it does: scans the new chunk, finds related chunks already in the brain, auto-suggests them for the related: field.

Why it matters: every chunk needs at least 1 link. Without this hook, you'll create orphan chunks that the brain can't find later.

HOOK 2

Validate Chunk Frontmatter

Triggers when: any .md file in .brain/ is saved

What it does: checks the file has all required fields (id, title, tags, date, related). If anything's missing, blocks the save and tells you what to fix.

Why it matters: garbage in, garbage out. This hook keeps your knowledge base clean from day 1.

HOOK 3

Auto-Save Learnings

Triggers when: the session ends

What it does: scans recent messages for keywords like "lesson", "pattern", "key insight" and dumps them to your memory inbox for review.

Why it matters: claude often discovers patterns mid-session. Without this hook, those patterns die when the session ends.

HOOK 4

Auto-Commit

Triggers when: claude finishes editing files and the session ends

What it does: runs git add . && git commit -m "auto-sync" so your brain always has version history.

Why it matters: if you ever need to roll back, you can. If your laptop dies, you don't lose 6 months of brain work.

⚡
The shift: once these hooks are running, your brain maintains itself. You write knowledge. The system organizes it. You commit nothing manually. The system commits for you. You forget to link chunks. The system reminds you. That's the difference between a knowledge base and a self-improving brain.

🏗️ 11. Compound Engineering — The 4-Phase Framework

most agency owners skip planning, jump to execution, then pay for it on the back end. compound engineering is a workflow for any task bigger than a day that forces you to invest where the leverage actually is.

the rule is simple. for any non-trivial task, allocate your time like this:
PLAN ████████████████ 40% WORK ████████ 20% REVIEW ████████ 20% DOCUMENT ████████ 20%
PHASE 1 — 40%

PLAN

most people give planning 5%. they get the mission wrong, the scope wrong, the order wrong. then they spend 3x longer fixing it during work. when you put 40% into planning, you understand the actual problem before lifting a finger. you catch dead ends in advance. you write the success criteria so you know when you're done.

PHASE 2 — 20%

WORK

once the plan is right, work is fast. you're executing a clear path. no decisions to make mid-flight. no second-guessing. 20% feels short. it is. that's the point. if work takes longer than 20%, your plan was incomplete.

PHASE 3 — 20%

REVIEW

you almost never get it right on the first pass. review is where you catch what work missed. read your output cold. pretend you didn't write it. find the holes. fix them. don't skip this. don't speed-run it.

PHASE 4 — 20%

DOCUMENT

this is the part everyone skips. big mistake. documenting is where the work compounds. you write down what you learned, what worked, what didn't. next time you face a similar task, you read your own notes and skip 70% of the planning phase. skip documenting and you'll redo this same work in 3 months.

🎯
When to use it: new client onboarding, launching a new ad funnel, building a new SOP, hiring decisions, pricing changes. Any project longer than 1 day. When NOT to use it: replying to an email, quick fixes, 30-min tasks. Match the framework to the size of the task.

🎯 12. Ralph Wiggum — The 8-Pass Copy Loop

most AI copy is 6/10 quality. good enough to look like work, not good enough to convert. the ralph wiggum technique forces the same copy through 8 framework passes. after each pass, the copy gets stronger. by pass 8, it's 9.5+.

it's named after ralph wiggum from the simpsons, who repeats things until they stick. same energy.

you wrote a sales email. it looks fine. you ship it. it does 1.2% conversion. you should have run it through ralph. same email, after ralph: 3.8% conversion. that's the difference between "good enough" copy and copy that actually pays you.
PASS 1

Schwartz Awareness Check

Is this written for the right awareness level? Are we leading with the problem (unaware) or the offer (most-aware)? Mismatch kills conversion.

PASS 2

Hook Strength Check

Does the first line stop the scroll? Would you keep reading if you didn't have to?

PASS 3

Specificity Check

Replace every vague word. "Many" → "73%". "Soon" → "by Friday". "Growth" → "+$8K MRR".

PASS 4

Proof Check

Every claim needs a number, name, or example next to it. If you can't add proof, cut the claim.

PASS 5

Voice Check

Does this sound like a human or like AI? Em dashes. Triple structures. Corporate words like "leverage", "robust", "seamless". Kill all of it.

PASS 6

Pace Check

Mix sentence length. Use fragments. Use line breaks. White space is a weapon.

PASS 7

Cut Check

Cut 20% without losing meaning. Adjectives, qualifiers, throat-clearing intros, redundant transitions. All filler.

PASS 8

Cold Read Test

Read it fresh. Does it flow? Does it land? Does it close? If anything feels off, loop back to whichever pass missed.

⚡
The cost: 8-25 minutes per piece. Most people won't do this. They'll write copy in 5 minutes and ship it. That's why most copy is mediocre. You'll do it. Your copy will hit. Your campaigns will print.
