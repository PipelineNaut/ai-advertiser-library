Lesson 4.5 — Module 4: Scaling

Building Systems — The 80/15/5 Rule

SOPs Systems ~20 min
The difference between a freelancer and a business owner is systems. A freelancer does everything from scratch. A business owner has SOPs, templates, and workflows that make everything repeatable. This lesson builds your entire operating system.

🧠 1. The 80/15/5 Rule

Here's how your work should be distributed when your systems are running:

80%
AI Does This
Ad copy, creative concepts, audience research, report drafts, optimization suggestions
15%
VA Does This
Uploads, formatting, screenshots, scheduling, file organization, data entry
5%
YOU Do This
Strategy decisions, client calls, approvals, quality control, new business

Your 5% is the highest-value work: Making decisions, talking to clients, and approving deliverables. Everything else should run without you touching it. That's how you manage 7 clients in 2-3 hours per day.

📋 2. The 5 SOPs You Need

SOP = Standard Operating Procedure. It's just a document that says "here's how we do X." You need exactly 5:

SOP 1: Client Onboarding
New client signs → Get business info → Set up Business Manager access → Install pixel → Create campaign structure → Launch first ads → Send welcome email with expectations.
Time to build: 30 min (you already did this in Module 3)
SOP 2: Campaign Launch
AI generates ad copy → AI generates creatives → VA uploads to Ads Manager → You review → Set targeting (location + broad) → Set budget → Launch → Monitor first 48 hours.
Time to build: 20 min
SOP 3: Weekly Optimization
VA takes screenshots → You review metrics → Kill ads with CPL 2x+ target → Duplicate winners with new audiences → Increase budget on winners by 20% max → Note changes in tracker.
Time to build: 20 min
SOP 4: Weekly & Monthly Reporting
VA fills in report template with numbers → You add insights and recommendations → VA formats and sends → Monthly: same but more detailed + strategy call prep.
Time to build: 15 min (templates from lessons 3.11 and 4.2)
SOP 5: Creative Refresh
Identify fatigued creatives (frequency 3+, CTR dropping) → AI generates 5-10 new ad copy variations → AI generates new images → VA uploads → You review and launch → Test for 3-5 days.
Time to build: 20 min
🖥️ SHOW ON SCREEN: Open Google Docs. Create a new doc titled "SOP: Weekly Optimization." Write out the steps live. Show how simple it is — just numbered steps with Loom links.

📂 3. Google Drive Folder Structure

Every client gets the same folder structure. Duplicate it, don't recreate it.

📁 Agency
📁 SOPs — All 5 SOP docs
📁 Templates — Report templates, proposal templates
📁 _CLIENT TEMPLATE — Duplicate this for each new client
📁 01-Onboarding — Contract, business info, access checklist
📁 02-Creatives — Ad images, videos, copy docs
📁 03-Reports — Weekly and monthly reports
📁 04-Assets — Logos, brand colors, fonts, photos
📁 05-Notes — Call notes, strategy docs
📁 Tony's Pizza — (duplicated from template)
📁 Bright Smile Dental — (duplicated from template)
📁 FitZone Gym — (duplicated from template)

The golden rule: Templates → Duplicate → Don't Recreate. Every new client should take 5 minutes to set up because you're just duplicating a folder and renaming it.

🎛️ 4. Your Operations Hub (Google Sheets)

You need ONE place to see everything at a glance. A simple Google Sheet with 3 tabs:

Tab 1: Client Dashboard
Columns: Client Name | Retainer | Start Date | Ad Spend | Next Report Due | Next Call | Status | Notes
Tab 2: Task Tracker
Columns: Task | Client | Assigned To (You/VA) | Due Date | Status (To Do / In Progress / Done)
Tab 3: Revenue Tracker
Columns: Month | Revenue | Expenses (VA, tools, ads) | Net Profit | # of Clients | Revenue Per Client

⚡ 5. A Real Workflow: Creative Refresh (Example)

Here's how the 80/15/5 rule works in practice for a creative refresh:

YOU
Notice CTR dropping → decide it's time for new creatives
⏱️ 2 minutes
↓
AI
Generate 10 new ad copy variations + 5 image concepts
⏱️ 10 minutes
↓
YOU
Review and pick the best 5 copy + 3 images
⏱️ 5 minutes
↓
VA
Upload to Ads Manager, set up ad variations, save to Drive
⏱️ 15 minutes
↓
YOU
Final review → hit publish
⏱️ 3 minutes
Total: 35 minutes. Your time: 10 minutes.
Without systems, this takes 2+ hours and it's all you.
